Stakeholder Interaction Game

For BCI Technology Development

Objective:

The game aims to immerse participants in the world of BCI technology development, allowing them to take on different stakeholder roles, face challenges, make decisions, and experience the consequences of their choices.

Setup:

The game environment resembles a board game. Each player selects a stakeholder role (e.g., Neuroscientist, Investors, Competitors, etc.). Each role comes with a unique set of cards that represent their specific interests, concerns, and potential conflicts.

Stakeholder Roles:

They have the highest influence over the direction of the company and have a high stake in its success.
They have put in funds and their interests align with the success of the project.
They have significant influence over the approval and use of the technology, but their direct stake in the financial success of Neuralink is lower.
Provide necessary technology or services to Neuralink but may not have a direct financial stake.
They may directly benefit or be impacted by the technology, but their individual influence over company decisions might be limited.
They provide a medical and scientific perspective to Neuralink's technology.
Even though their primary focus might be their own interests, they have a keen interest in the dynamics and successes of Neurallink.

Gameplay:

  • Scenario Cards: At the start of each round, a scenario card is drawn. This card describes a situation or challenge related to BCI technology development.
  • Decision Time: Players use their role-specific cards to decide how they would react to the scenario based on their stakeholder's interests and concerns. They must also verbalize their reasoning.
  • Negotiation Phase: Players discuss and negotiate to find a solution that would satisfy the majority of stakeholders. They can use their cards to persuade, compromise, or form alliances.

Scenarios:

News reports suggest that a popular BCI product has vulnerabilities that could expose users' brain data to breaches.
A new technology allows BCI devices to integrate with AI, allowing users to "think" commands rather than physically issuing them. However, this brings up privacy and ethical concerns as the AI can "learn" from a user's thought patterns.
The latest BCI technology offers unprecedented accuracy and features, but it comes at a very high cost, making it unaffordable for many.
BCI technology is met with skepticism in certain cultures and communities as it's seen as "intervening" with the human brain and consciousness.
